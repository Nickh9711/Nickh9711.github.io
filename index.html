Teaching

How to Solve It (G.Polya, 1945) introduced a four-phased method to solve any mathematical problem. The four phases are understanding the problem, devising a plan, carrying out the plan, and looking back. I am working to adapt this method when teaching mathematics. "What is the definition?" is the first question I will always ask when looking at a mathematical problem. Learning mathematics requires the efforts from both the student and the teacher. The teacher will offer paths to students and keep them highly motivated on the way, but the students should be responsible for their own studies. If you want to see more about my thoughts on the four-phased method, you can check out my summary essay.

Emotions in using Intuition-Modelling-Computation

"Intuition-Modelling-Computation" is a method that I introduced to my MAT235 tut0401 class to solve any given multivariable calculus problem before I read How to Solve It. Surprisingly, it matches the first three phases of the method introduced by G. Polya.
