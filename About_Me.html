Nick Huang

My name is Nick (Zhiyong) Huang. I am a graduate teacher candidate at the University of Toronto, Ontario Institute for Studies in Education (OISE), pursuing my Masters in Teaching.

Teaching

How to Solve It (G.Polya, 1945) introduced a four-phased method to solve any mathematical problem. The four phases are understanding the problem, devising a plan, carrying out the plan, and looking back. I am working to adapt this method when teaching mathematics. "What is the definition?" is the first question I will always ask when looking at a mathematical problem. Learning mathematics requires the efforts from both the student and the teacher. The teacher will offer paths to students and keep them highly motivated on the way, but the students should be responsible for their own studies. If you want to see more about my philosophy of teaching, you can check out my teaching statement and the teaching page.

I have experiences teaching the following courses in Mathematics as a teaching assistant in university: Linear Algebra I, Linear Algebra II, Linear Optimization, Real Analysis, Differetial Equation, Multivariable Calculus.

I have experiences teaching the following courses as a teacher in Ontario high schools: Calculus and vectors, advanced functions, physics.
