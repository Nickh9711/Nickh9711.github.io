MATB24H3 TUT5 Linear Algebra II, Summer 2021

Welcome to the homepage for MATB24 tutorial sections TUT5 at the University of Toronto Scarborough, summer 2021. Tutorial notes will be posted here. Important announcements and the zoom link will be posted on the Quercus page. The course description from the UTSC calendar can be found here.

The main purpose of the tutorial is to help you understand the concepts that are taught in lectures by briefly going over the concepts again and doing practice problems. In particular, I will focus on improving your proof writing skills and the understanding of the definitions. Our recent research for math education in linear algebra shows a significant correlation between understanding definition and writing a proof in linear algebra. More information about the research can be found in the home page.

The four-phased method introduced by G.Polya in his book How to Solve It at 1945 will be applied when teaching. The method will provide you with a step-by-step guidances in approaching to solve a mathematical problem. A summary of the method can be found in the Wikipedia page here . More information about my teaching philosophy can be found in the home page.

How to academically succeed in this course:
Understand the concepts and algorithms by proving not just seeing. Do the practice problem even though some of them are relatively easy. Practice your proof writing skill and be precise in your proof. The hypothesis and conclusion should always be easily identified in your proof.
Tutorial Notes:
The tutorial notes will be posted here before the tutorial and the annotated note will be updated after the class. We may not cover all the materials in the tutorial notes.
  • Tutorial.1: Vector spaces and various proof techniques May.13 2021 (annotated note)
    • In this tutorial, we leanred how to prove basis property of a vector space using the axioms from the definition, and how to prove uniqueness of an element with the given condition. We saw some examples of vector spaces and review the ideas of direct proof and proof by cases.
  • Tutorial.2: Linear independent and spanning set May.20 2021 (annotated note)
    • In this tutorial, we proved an equivalent definition of linearly dependent, and discuss the difference between a logical conjunction and a logical implication to better understand the definition of linearly independent. We saw some examples of how to prove linearly independence of a given set of vectors in a vector space. We reviewed the computational approaches to check linearly independence and spanning set, and understand the logic behind the computations.
  • LaTeX seminar: tex file and pdf file May.23 2021 (updated 12:23pm May.23 2021)
  • Tutorial.3: Linear transformation and its matrix representation May.27 2021 (annotated note)
    • In this tutorial, we leanred the idea of coordinate vector and matrix representation, and invertibility of matrices. We saw an example of how a given linearly independent assumption should be properly used in the proof. Besides, we discussed the relation between a given linear transformation and its matrix representation.
