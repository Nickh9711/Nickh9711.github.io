Important announcements and the zoom link will be posted on the Quercus page. The course description from the UTSC calendar can be found here. While the emphasize of the course is computation, you should have a general idea of the concept in order to correct do the computation. There are four phases to follow when solving a problem in multivariable Calculus, intuition-modelling-computation-look_back. More information about the method (which is a modification of the four phases method introduced by G.Polya in his book How to Solve It at 1945) and myself can be found in the home page.
  • Intuition: Understand what the question is asking and draw a picture to visualize the question, if possible.
  • Modelling: Set up the mathematical model using the suitable notation to solve the problem using your intuition.
  • Computation: This is when you do the computation, maybe with some useful techniques or algorithms. When and how can you use those techniques and/or algorithms?
  • Look back: Does your final answer make sense given your intuition? Did your model miss any hidden information? Did you make a computational mistake?
